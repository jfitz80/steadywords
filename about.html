About

This wasn’t built from optimism. It was built from exhaustion.

SteadyWords started with one very tired human who was trying to hold work, family, grief, and everyday life together — and couldn’t find any space that felt honest.

Most affirmation apps felt wrong. They were full of rainbow quotes and “you’re unstoppable” slogans that didn’t match what real life looked like: pressure at work, kids struggling, grief that doesn’t go away, and the constant fear of dropping something important.

SteadyWords grew out of that gap — from the feeling of being unseen at work, overwhelmed at home, and quietly carrying more than anyone realised. It’s built for people who are still showing up, even when they feel empty.

This isn’t a place that demands you “stay positive”. It’s a place that says: “Of course you’re tired. It makes sense. You’re allowed to stop pretending you’re okay for a minute.”

Who SteadyWords is for

If any of this sounds like you, you’re in the right place.

  • You feel sad or flat more often than you’d like, but still drag yourself through the day.
  • You’re supporting other people — kids, partner, family, colleagues — and rarely have space for your own feelings.
  • You’re scared of burning out or losing your job, even though your work no longer feels meaningful.
  • You’re tired of advice that assumes you have loads of free time, energy, or money to “fix” everything.
  • You secretly just want someone to say, “I see how hard you’re trying. It makes sense you feel this way.”

What SteadyWords believes

Behind every feature is a simple set of beliefs about what exhausted humans actually need.

Validation first, advice later

Most of us don’t need motivational speeches. We need someone to say, “What you’re feeling makes sense,” before anything else.

Tiny is better than nothing

Big life overhauls feel impossible when you’re drained. A 20-second pause, one kind thought, or a single deep breath is enough to count as care.

You shouldn’t have to perform being okay

You deserve at least one corner of your day where you don’t have to put on a brave face — where you can be honest without being judged.

Being tired doesn’t mean you’re failing

Exhaustion isn’t a moral weakness. It’s a signal. SteadyWords exists to listen to that signal and respond with gentleness, not shame.

If any part of this feels like your life, SteadyWords was built with you in mind.
